What Are Product Safety Alerts?
About Safety Alerts

Product safety alerts are official government notifications issued when a consumer product poses a risk to health, safety, or the environment. The EU publishes via Safety Gate (RAPEX); the UK via the Office for Product Safety & Standards (OPSS).

Each alert details the product, its hazard, origin, and the enforcement action taken — from voluntary recalls to compulsory import bans — and is shared across authorities to coordinate market surveillance.

Regulations & Directives

Flagged products typically fail one or more of these frameworks:

General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR) REACH Regulation (chemicals) Toy Safety Directive (2009/48/EC) Low Voltage Directive (LVD) Radio Equipment Directive (RED) RoHS Directive Cosmetics Regulation (EC 1223/2009) Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC) EN 71 Toy Safety Standards UK Product Safety & Metrology Regs
Common Recall Reasons
Excessive chemicals — banned substances (phthalates, heavy metals, lilial, asbestos) above permitted limits
Choking & entrapment — small detachable parts or gaps posing risks to young children
Electric shock & fire — faulty insulation, overheating batteries, or non-compliant wiring
Microbiological contamination — unsafe bacteria or mould in cosmetics or personal care
Missing documentation — absent CE/UKCA marks, no conformity declaration, or missing instructions
Counterfeiting — brand impersonation without meeting the originals' safety standards

Chemical risk dominates
560 alerts (41%) cite chemical hazards — concentrated in cosmetics (344), toys (68), jewellery (49), and clothing (38). REACH Regulation non-compliance is the most cited rule.
Toys: choking & chemical
Choking is the #1 toy hazard (113 alerts), followed by chemical (50). February 2026 was the busiest single month with 322 total alerts.

Key UK patterns & trends
Asbestos: the dominant story
39 alerts (14.5% of all UK alerts) relate to asbestos contamination in craft sand and toy kits — an unprecedented cluster not seen in EU data at this scale.
Fire & electrical #1 hazard
99 fire/burn risk alerts and 34 electric shock alerts — together 49% of all UK alerts. Electrical appliances are the top category with 42 alerts.
Toy hazards evolving
UK toy alerts are dominated by novel physical hazards — expanding polymer ingestion (35 alerts) — rather than the chemical toxicity focus seen in EU toy alerts.
China: 67% origin share
181 of 269 UK flagged products originate from China — 11 percentage points higher than the EU's 56% share, reflecting different import mix post-Brexit.
Import bans = top action
The UK's most common enforcement action (112 cases) is an import ban — a more aggressive posture than EU's split between compulsory and voluntary measures.
TikTok Shop on radar
4 UK alerts explicitly name TikTok Shop as the sales platform — a new enforcement signal that online marketplace monitoring is increasing.

Shared across both markets
China dominates origin
Both markets see China as the majority source of flagged products — 56% in the EU, 67% in the UK. This reflects the volume of low-cost consumer goods entering both markets from Chinese manufacturers.
Electrical & toy risk universal
Electrical appliances and toys are top-flagged categories in both markets. Electric shock, fire, and choking hazards appear consistently regardless of regulatory framework.
Online marketplace exposure
Both markets are grappling with unsafe products sold via online platforms. The UK now explicitly names platforms (TikTok Shop, Amazon) in recall notices — the EU is likely to follow.
