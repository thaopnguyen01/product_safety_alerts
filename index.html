What Are Product Safety Alerts?
About Safety Alerts

Product safety alerts are official government notifications issued when a consumer product is found to pose a risk to health, safety, or the environment. They are published through the EU's Safety Gate (RAPEX) system and the UK's Office for Product Safety and Standards (OPSS).

Each alert describes the product, the hazard it presents, its origin, and the enforcement action taken — ranging from voluntary recalls to compulsory import bans. Alerts are shared across EU member states and with trading partners to coordinate market surveillance.

Regulations & Directives

Products flagged in these alerts typically fail to comply with one or more of the following EU/UK frameworks:

General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR) REACH Regulation (chemicals) Toy Safety Directive (2009/48/EC) Low Voltage Directive (LVD) Radio Equipment Directive (RED) RoHS Directive (hazardous substances) PPE Regulation (EU 2016/425) Cosmetics Regulation (EC 1223/2009) Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC) EN 71 Toy Safety Standards
Common Recall Reasons
Excessive chemicals — banned substances (e.g. bisphenol A, phthalates, heavy metals) above permitted limits in cosmetics, toys, jewellery, or clothing
Choking & entrapment — small detachable parts, cords, or gaps in toys and childcare products that pose risks to young children
Electric shock & fire — faulty insulation, missing CE marks, or non-compliant wiring in electrical appliances and lighting
Microbiological contamination — unsafe levels of bacteria or mould in cosmetics or personal care products
Missing safety documentation — no declaration of conformity, absent CE/UKCA marking, or no instructions in the local language
Counterfeiting — products impersonating legitimate brands without meeting the safety standards of the originals

Key patterns & trends
Chemical risk dominates
560 alerts (41%) cite chemical hazards — concentrated in cosmetics (344), toys (68), jewellery (49), and clothing (38). REACH Regulation non-compliance is the most cited rule.
Toys: choking & chemical
Choking is the #1 toy hazard (113 alerts), followed by chemical (50). February 2026 was the single busiest month overall, with 322 total alerts across all categories.
Counterfeit pattern
15 confirmed counterfeits. Pop Mart drove 9 alerts — the largest single brand impersonation cluster in the dataset.
France & UK are top reporters
France (206) and UK (191) together make up 29% of all alerts. Italy (174) and Czechia (149) also rank highly among reporting nations.
Electrical: fire & shock
217 electrical appliance alerts — electric shock (141) and fire (70) are the primary risks. Most are consumer products originating from China.
Growing alert volume
Volume climbed from 166 in December to a peak of 322 in February, then stabilized around 250–300/month. Consumer products account for 94% of all alerts.
