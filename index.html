What Are Product Safety Alerts?
About Safety Alerts

Product safety alerts are official government notifications issued when a consumer product poses a risk to health, safety, or the environment. The EU publishes via Safety Gate (RAPEX); the UK via the Office for Product Safety & Standards (OPSS).

Each alert details the product, its hazard, origin, and the enforcement action taken — from voluntary recalls to compulsory import bans — and is shared across authorities to coordinate market surveillance.

Regulations & Directives

Flagged products typically fail one or more of these frameworks:

General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR) REACH Regulation (chemicals) Toy Safety Directive (2009/48/EC) Low Voltage Directive (LVD) Radio Equipment Directive (RED) RoHS Directive Cosmetics Regulation (EC 1223/2009) Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC) EN 71 Toy Safety Standards UK Product Safety & Metrology Regs
Common Recall Reasons
Excessive chemicals — banned substances (phthalates, heavy metals, lilial, asbestos) above permitted limits
Choking & entrapment — small detachable parts or gaps posing risks to young children
Electric shock & fire — faulty insulation, overheating batteries, or non-compliant wiring
Microbiological contamination — unsafe bacteria or mould in cosmetics or personal care
Missing documentation — absent CE/UKCA marks, no conformity declaration, or missing instructions
Counterfeiting — brand impersonation without meeting the originals' safety standards

Key UK patterns & trends
Asbestos: the dominant story
39 alerts (12.5% of all UK alerts) relate to asbestos contamination in craft sand and toy kits — an unprecedented cluster absent from EU data at comparable scale.
Fire & electrical #1 hazard
118 fire/burn alerts and 35 electric shock alerts — together 49% of all UK alerts. Electrical appliances are the top category at 56 alerts.
Toy hazards evolving
UK toy alerts are dominated by novel physical hazards — expanding polymer ingestion (35 alerts) — rather than the chemical toxicity focus seen in EU toy data.
China: 69% origin share
214 of 312 UK flagged products originate from China — 10 percentage points higher than EU's 59% share, reflecting post-Brexit import mix shifts.
Import bans = top action
129 import bans (41%) is the UK's most common enforcement action — a more aggressive border-focused posture compared to the EU's compulsory/voluntary split.
Machinery alerts rising
Machinery surged to the #3 category in Q2 (16 alerts vs 7 in Q1, +129%). UK-origin products represent 9 of 16 Q2 machinery alerts — a domestic compliance concern.

Q1 EU: Electrical appliances at 93 alerts — primarily chargers and small appliances with electric shock risk.
Electrical
trend
Q2 EU: Electrical surged to 142 alerts — a 56% increase, making it the single largest Q2 category. Fire risk alerts grew notably alongside electric shock cases, pointing to a broader product quality issue in consumer electronics.

What to watch in Q3
EU electrical surge
The 56% Q2 increase in EU electrical alerts — particularly fire-risk products — warrants close monitoring in Q3. If the trend continues, electrical appliances could become the dominant EU category for the full year.
Hong Kong routing (EU)
The 6× jump in Hong Kong-origin EU alerts in Q2 is a significant signal. If confirmed in Q3, this suggests a systematic supply chain evasion pattern that regulators will likely respond to directly.
UK domestic manufacturing
The rise of UK-origin machinery alerts in Q2 is new — historically, UK alerts focus on imported goods. Increased HSE activity suggests domestic compliance audits are intensifying heading into Q3.
